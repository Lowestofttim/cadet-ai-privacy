Cadet AI — Privacy Policy

Study aid for UK Army Cadet Force (ACF) & Combined Cadet Force (CCF) cadets

Last updated: 6 June 2026

Cadet AI (“the app”, “we”, “us”, “our”) is a study aid for UK Army Cadet Force (ACF) and Combined Cadet Force (CCF) cadets. We take the privacy of young people seriously and have written this policy to be clear and honest about what we collect and why. This app is not an official MOD, ACF, CCF or Cadet Forces product.

Operator: Cadet AI  ·  Privacy contact: timyoungman75@gmail.com

1. Who this app is for, and a note for parents/guardians

Cadet AI is intended for cadets aged 13 and over. If you are under 13, UK law requires a parent or guardian to approve your account. When you sign up we ask your age; cadets under 13 are asked to involve a parent or guardian before continuing.

Parents/guardians can contact us at the address above to see, correct, or delete their child’s data at any time.

2. What we collect

We only collect what the app needs to work:

  • Account details — your email address (via Google sign-in or email/password sign-up). We never see or store your Google password.
  • Profile you create — display name, cadet organisation (ACF/CCF), section (e.g. Army/RAF), rank, star level, and the unit or school you select.
  • Age check — a record that you confirmed you are old enough to use the app (we store a yes/no “age verified” flag, not your date of birth).
  • Your progress — quiz results, scores, XP, badges, awards, streaks, and rank.
  • Messages to TANGO — the questions you type to the in-app AI assistant and its replies, so the feature can answer you.
  • App settings — your preferences (theme, reminders, leaderboard opt-in, etc.).

We do not collect your location, contacts, photos, or device identifiers for advertising. The app has no advertising SDKs and no third-party analytics trackers, and we do not sell your data.

3. Microphone

If you use TANGO’s optional voice/dictation feature, the app uses your device microphone to convert your speech to text on your device. You are asked for microphone permission first, and you can decline or revoke it; the rest of the app still works.

4. How we use your data

  • To create and secure your account and sign you in.
  • To save and show your training progress, badges, awards, and rank.
  • To answer your questions in the TANGO assistant.
  • To show leaderboards only if you choose to appear on them (see §6).
  • To send you the optional daily streak reminder if you turn it on.

Legal basis (UK GDPR): performing our agreement with you to provide the app (Article 6(1)(b)), and your consent for optional features such as leaderboards and reminders (Article 6(1)(a)), which you can withdraw at any time in Settings.

5. Who we share it with (our processors)

We use a small number of trusted service providers to run the app. They process data on our behalf under their own security and privacy terms:

  • Supabase — secure hosting, database, and authentication for your account and progress data.
  • Google — only if you choose “Continue with Google”, to sign you in. Google tells us your email address and basic profile; we do not post anything to your Google account.
  • OpenAI — the messages you send to the TANGO assistant are sent to OpenAI to generate a reply. Do not type sensitive personal information into TANGO.

We do not share your personal data with anyone else, and we never sell it.

6. Leaderboards

Leaderboards are off by default. If you turn on “Appear on leaderboards” in Settings, your display name, unit/school, and XP can be shown to other cadets in national and unit rankings. You can turn this off at any time, and we will stop including you.

7. How long we keep it

We keep your account and progress data while your account exists. If you ask us to delete your account, we delete your personal data (we may keep minimal records where the law requires). Messages to TANGO are retained only as long as needed to provide and improve the feature.

8. Your rights

Under UK GDPR you can ask us to: access a copy of your data; correct it; delete it; restrict or object to how we use it; or receive it in a portable format. To make a request, email the privacy contact above. You also have the right to complain to the UK Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) at https://ico.org.uk.

9. Security

Data is transmitted over encrypted connections (HTTPS) and stored by our hosting provider with access controls. No system is perfectly secure, but we take reasonable steps to protect your information.

10. Changes to this policy

If we change this policy we will update the “Last updated” date above and, for significant changes, notify you in the app.

11. Contact

Questions, requests, or concerns: timyoungman75@gmail.com.
